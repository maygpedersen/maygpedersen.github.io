Organisation

MESA Down Under

17th-21st June 2024, Winter School, University of Sydney, Australia

The stellar structure and evolution code Modules for Experiments in Stellar Astrophysics (MESA) was identified as a key software tool for astronomy in the 2020 U.S. Decadal Survey. It has played a pivotal role in making the study of stellar structure and evolution readily accessible to the broad astronomy community largely due to its open-source nature and annual summer schools dedicated to teaching new users how to use the code. In 2024 I brought the school to Australia for the first time under the name MESA Down Under, making the school more accessible to early career researchers (MSc, PhD, postdocs) in Australia who made up 40% of the participants. The teaching team for the school included MESA developers, who taught the participants the basics of how to use the code, and international experts in a variety of fields in astrophysics who provided example science applications of the code. The hands-on exercises were developed by the lecturers and a team of 13 teaching assistants from nine different countries under the supervision of the Teaching Organization Committee that I led and established for the school.
